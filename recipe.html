Jollof Rice(Nigerian Style)

Jollof Rice is a vibrant and flavorful West African dish made by cooking rice in a rich blend of tomatoes, peppers, onions, and spices. In the Nigerian style, it’s often smoky, slightly spicy, and served with fried plantain, chicken, beef, or fish. Loved for its bold taste and beautiful orange-red color, it’s a staple at parties, family gatherings, and celebrations.

Recipe Information

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a one-pot dish that is often served at parties and special occasions. It is known for its rich, smoky flavor and vibrant color.

Preparation Time

Prep time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time

Cook time: 45 minutes

Number of Servings

Serves: 6

Difficulty Level

intermediate

Nutrition Facts

  • Calories: 350 per serving
  • Protein: 6g
  • Carbohydrates: 60g
  • Fat: 10g
  • Sodium: 300mg

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 medium tomatoes, blended
  • 1 red bell pepper, blended
  • 2-3 Scotch bonnet peppers, blended (adjust for spice level)
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: protein of choice (chicken, beef, or fish)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the blended tomatoes, red bell pepper, and Scotch bonnet peppers to the pot. Cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens and the oil begins to separate.
  4. Stir in the thyme, curry powder, and salt. Add the drained rice and mix well to coat the rice with the tomato mixture.
  5. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, ensuring the rice is submerged. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot.
  6. Cook for 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed. Avoid opening the lid frequently.
  7. Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork and let it sit for 5 minutes before serving.

Cooking tips

  1. Use Long-Grain Parboiled Rice

    2. Long-grain parboiled rice is preferred for Jollof rice because it holds its shape well and absorbs flavors without becoming mushy.

  2. Fry the Tomato-Pepper Base Well

    3. Ensure the tomato-pepper base is well-fried to enhance the flavor and achieve the desired richness.

  3. Season Generously

    4. Don’t be afraid to add salt and spices to the rice. The flavors should be bold and well-balanced.

  4. Control the Water

    5. Be mindful of the liquid-to-rice ratio. Too much water can make the rice mushy, while too little can result in undercooked rice.

  5. Cook on Low Heat for the “Party Jollof” Smoky Flavor

    6. For a smokier flavor, you can cook the rice over a low flame and let it "burn" slightly at the bottom.

  6. Use Stock Instead of Plain Water

    7. Using chicken or vegetable stock instead of plain water adds depth and richness to the dish.

  7. Don’t Stir Too Often

    8. Once you add the rice to the pot, avoid stirring too much. This helps prevent the rice from becoming mushy and allows it to cook evenly.

  8. Let It Rest

    9. After cooking, let the Jollof rice rest for a few minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld and the rice to firm up slightly.
