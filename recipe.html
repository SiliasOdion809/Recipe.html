Nigerian Jollof Rice Recipe

Jollof Rice is a vibrant and flavorful West African dish made by cooking rice in a rich blend of tomatoes, peppers, onions, and spices. In the Nigerian style, it’s often smoky, slightly spicy, and served with fried plantain, chicken, beef, or fish. Loved for its bold taste and beautiful orange-red color, it’s a staple at parties, family gatherings, and celebrations.

Recipe Information

Nigerian Jollof Rice is one of West Africa’s most celebrated dishes, known for its vibrant red-orange color and irresistible smoky flavor. It is prepared by simmering rice in a rich tomato and pepper sauce seasoned with onions, garlic, ginger, and aromatic spices. This dish strikes a perfect balance between sweetness from ripe tomatoes and a mild heat from fresh chilies. Often served with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or fish, Jollof Rice is a staple at weddings, birthdays, and festive gatherings, bringing people together with its delightful taste and inviting aroma.

  • Preparation Time: 15 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 45-60 minutes
  • Number of Servings: 4-6 People
  • Difficulty Level:Moderate – requires careful seasoning, heat control, and patience to achieve the signature smoky “party Jollof” flavor without burning the rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, blended
  • 1 green bell pepper, blended
  • 4 ripe tomatoes, blended
  • 2-3 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional protein: chicken, beef, or fish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the blended red and green bell peppers, and sauté for about 5 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  4. Stir in the blended tomatoes, thyme, curry powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 10-15 minutes, allowing the sauce to reduce and the flavors to meld.
  5. Add the soaked and drained rice to the pot, stirring to coat the grains with the sauce.
  6. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, ensuring the rice is submerged. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  7. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook for 25-30 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. If using, add the protein of your choice during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  9. Once cooked, remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 10 minutes.
  10. Fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot, garnished with fried plantains or your choice of protein.

Tips

  • For a smoky flavor, you can cook the rice over low heat for a longer time or use a charcoal grill to infuse a smoky aroma.
  • Adjust the spice level by adding more or fewer chilies according to your taste.
  • Serve with fried plantains, coleslaw, or a side salad for a complete meal.

Enjoy your delicious Nigerian Jollof Rice!
